A Brief Overview of the Material

In January 2020, more than five decades after his death, 1,133 letters written by Eliot to Emily Hale were unsealed in the archives of the Firestone Library in Princeton University. The unsealing of these letters marked the culmination of one of the longest-running literary mysteries and proved to be a watershed moment in the history of Eliot studies. Since then, these letters have inspired excitement and consternation in equal measure, helping shape a compelling narrative about Eliot’s romantic association with Hale, and the effect that it had on his poetry. “It is the missing piece of his career and certainly the most significant revelation about a major poet of the 20th century that we’ve had,” Tony Cuda, director of the T.S. Eliot International Summer School in London, told the Guardian. As many scholars have pointed out, these letters may well be considered the final and most important piece in the jigsaw puzzle in our efforts to understand Eliot as a poet.

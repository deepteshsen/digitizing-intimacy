About the Project

This project transforms the 1,133 letters written by T.S. Eliot to Emily Hale into a computational research environment. Inspired by Mapping the Republic of Letters, it integrates corpus linguistics, network theory, and longitudinal visualization to uncover structural patterns of intimacy and intellectual transformation across four decades.

Methodology

Corpus Preparation

Letters cleaned, tokenized, and structured with metadata (date, location, themes).

Topic Modeling

LDA-based modeling to detect recurring themes (religion, guilt, devotion, artistic labour).

Network Analysis

Named entity extraction and co-occurrence modeling using graph theory metrics.

Temporal Analysis

Sentiment scoring and longitudinal modeling aligned with major biographical milestones.

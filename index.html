Digitizing Intimacy

A Computational and Network-Based Modelling of the T.S. Eliot–Emily Hale Correspondence (1930–1969)

Overview

This project reconceptualises the Eliot–Hale correspondence (1930–1969) as a dynamic computational archive inspired by the Republic of Letters initiative. It combines corpus linguistics, topic modelling, network analysis, and temporal visualisation to examine patterns of intimacy, devotion, and intellectual transformation across four decades.

Computational Analysis

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) identifies recurring thematic clusters including religious reflection, marital conflict, emotional dependence, artistic labour, illness, and spiritual guilt. Sentiment modelling and lexical frequency analysis track tonal shifts over time.

Tools & Techniques

Voyant Tools enables lexical frequency and collocation analysis. Named Entity Recognition (NER) extracts references to persons, institutions, and places. Gephi and D3.js construct weighted co-occurrence networks. Topic modelling is implemented through probabilistic LDA frameworks. Temporal structuring aligns textual shifts with major biographical and literary milestones. All datasets are version-controlled to ensure transparency and reproducibility.

Budget Framework

Funding supports research assistance for corpus cleaning and metadata tagging, technical development of interactive visualisations, server hosting, and dissemination. The project relies primarily on open-source infrastructure, ensuring modest costs and long-term sustainability.

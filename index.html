Digitizing Intimacy

A Computational and Network-Based Modelling of the T.S. Eliot–Emily Hale Correspondence (1930–1969)

Overview Timeline Analysis Network Tools & Techniques Budget Eliot–Hale Letters

Overview

This project reconceptualises the Eliot–Hale correspondence (1930–1969) as a dynamic computational archive inspired by the Republic of Letters initiative. It combines corpus linguistics, topic modelling, network analysis, and temporal visualisation to reveal structural patterns of intimacy, devotion, and intellectual transformation across four decades.

Chronology

Computational Analysis

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) identifies recurring thematic clusters including religious reflection, emotional dependence, artistic labour, and marital conflict. Sentiment tracking enables longitudinal measurement of tonal shifts.

Relational Network

This force-directed network models co-occurrence relationships among individuals and institutions referenced within the correspondence.

Tools & Techniques

Voyant Tools enables lexical frequency and collocation analysis. Named Entity Recognition extracts references to persons and institutions. Gephi and D3.js construct weighted co-occurrence networks. LDA modelling identifies thematic clusters whose prevalence is plotted chronologically. Timeline-based structuring aligns textual shifts with biographical and literary milestones.

Budget Framework

Funding supports research assistance, corpus cleaning, server hosting, technical development, and dissemination. Built primarily with open-source infrastructure to ensure sustainability and reproducibility.
